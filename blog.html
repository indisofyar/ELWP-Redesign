We’re loving spending time with you, so we’re extending

  • ELWP Team
  • Jan 1, 2022

We’re so proud of what we’ve achieved together. We’ve dreamed an audacious dream and we’ve raised over £200,000 in a little over a month to make that dream come true. That’s nothing short of amazing.

Over the last week or so we’ve received a lot of feedback from people who love the project but haven’t managed to donate yet. And we get it; there’s an awful lot going on at the moment. So we’ve spoken to Crowdfunder and we’re extending the deadline to reach our goal. We now have 84 days to reach our target!

As ever, please do continue to talk to your networks about the project and encourage them to donate what they can: https://www.crowdfunder.co.uk/p/elwp. And do come and join us at Today Bread in Walthamstow on Friday 12th August at 19:00, for a short presentation and a Q&A, to find out about the project in more detail, ask all your burning questions, and learn what you can do to help get us reach £500,000: https://www.facebook.com/events/460496495684028/.

Finally, if you’d like to get involved with the project or know of a company that has a strong approach to corporate social responsibility and might be interested in supporting us, please get in touch: nathan.miller@elwp.org.uk.

And, of course, please do continue to get the word out there via social media by following and sharing our posts: Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn.

Thank you, from the East London Waterworks Park team.

The awesomeness of community: a July update from East London Waterworks Park

  • Abigail Woodman
  • July, 2022

I’ve just been talking to someone who said that the most inspiring thing about East London Waterworks Park is its focus on community, and so I decided to look up the definition of ‘community’. Broadly, it is:

  1. a group of people living in the same place or having a particular characteristic in common
  2. the condition of sharing or having certain attitudes and interests in common
  3. a group of interdependent plants or animals growing or living together in natural conditions or occupying a specified habitat.

How cool to discover that East London Waterworks Park is all three of these things: it is a group of people living in Waltham Forest and Hackney; it is a group of people that share a desire to create a beautiful place where people can reconnect with nature; and it is a group of people that are growing together to develop a new way of doing business that is inclusive and supportive and responsive to the needs of our environment.

Just in case it’s passed you by: East London Waterworks Park wants to buy the Thames Water depot for the community so that it is owned by the community and the community decides how it is managed and what happens there. And you are all welcome to be part of that community, now or in the future, whenever you have the time and space and energy. That’s lots of ‘and’s because the project is all about adding and joining and making things better for us all and the animals and plants we share our home with.

The crowdfunder has been doing well. I hope, by the time I post this blog, we’ll have reached £200,000. That’s a huge amount of money to have raised in a few short weeks, and thank you to everyone that has contributed so far. We are really and truly grateful. But this is an all-or-nothing crowdfunder. That means, if we don’t reach £500,000 by 23 August, we won’t receive anything. Now, we’re not panicking. We do not do panic! But we do need to call on the awesomeness of community for its – your – help.

  • If you haven’t donated yet, please do it now: www.crowdfunder.co.uk/p/elwp.
  • If you know of anyone who has expressed an interest in the project but hasn’t donated, please give them a nudge: www.crowdfunder.co.uk/p/elwp.
  • If you haven’t shared the project with everyone you know yet, please do so now: www.crowdfunder.co.uk/p/elwp.
  • And if you work for a company that has a strong approach to corporate social responsibility and might be interested in supporting us, please put us in touch: nathan.miller@elwp.org.uk.

I absolutely appreciate that times are tough and if you cannot afford to donate or are working so hard to make ends meet that you don’t have the head space to think about the project, don’t give it a second thought. Know that your community puts its arms around you and is doing its best to create a space that you and your friends and family can enjoy without thinking about money.
