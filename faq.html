The East London Waterworks Park is a community group idea to buy the 5.68-hectare Thames Water Depot on Lea Bridge Road in Waltham Forest and transform it into a publicly accessible green space with natural swimming ponds.

We hope that you’ll enjoy visiting the park to connect with nature, go wild swimming and volunteer for conservation activities. Research has shown that access to green and blue spaces makes people mentally healthier and volunteering provides opportunities for those who are socially isolated or seeking purpose. We’ll work with small local businesses and individuals where possible to grow the local green economy.

We’re also reaching out to people who are often underrepresented in projects like these to ensure that East London Waterworks Park is inclusive and truly welcoming to everyone.

The site will develop as a circular economy, with rainwater and greywater collected and reused. Reintroducing open water and rewilding the site will also improve biodiversity and air quality and we will generate all the power we need on site and export renewable energy to the grid.

We’re currently fundraising to purchase the land, which is owned by the Secretary of State for Communities and Local Government (now the Secretary of State for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities), and managed by LocatED, the Department for Education’s arms-length property company. So far, we’ve raised almost £60,000 from individual donations and you can help us by donating to our crowdfunder today.

We plan to be operational by 2029.

You can help us by donating to our crowdfunder today, or if you’re keen to get involved more generally as a volunteer then please get in touch.
