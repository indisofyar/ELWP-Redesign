There is a lot of work involved in getting the East London Waterwork Park project up and running! If you have any specific skills that you think would be useful, or if you are just keen to get involved more generally, we would love to hear from you.

Circles

Before getting in touch, you will need to understand a little about how we are organised. Our day-to-day work is carried out by circles. All of our members belong to a circle (most belong to just one circle, but you can belong to more if you wish). These are the circles that we currently have.

Governance circle

Learning circle

Design circle

Inclusivity circle

Communications circle

Fundraising circle

Technology circle

Governance circle

The governance circle is all about ensuring the East London Waterworks Park has an organisational structure and culture that blend the warmth and camaraderie of a grassroots campaign with the professionalism of a business that will achieve its aims and objectives. It is currently working on a business plan and registering as a charity.

Learning circle

The learning circle is the lead learner in a life-long collaborative learning journey. It is working on ensuring that all learning experiences in and through the project and park adhere to our learning design principles, enabling our community to learn how to live harmoniously with nature. If you are interested in desk research related to learning experience design, and community, conservation and nature-based learning, or if you are interested in changing education paradigms, we'd love to hear from you.

Design circle

The design circle is working with civil and environmental engineers to test the feasibility of our ideas and help us develop the concept. It is thinking about a water strategy, an energy strategy, a circular economy for the site and a strategy for the concrete sub-structures. If you have experience in or are studying architecture, ecology, civic engineering, and/or urban design, we should be especially interested in hearing from you.

Inclusivity circle

The inclusivity circle focuses on ensuring that everybody feels welcome in and around the project and that the East London Waterworks Park will be designed to welcome everybody. Currently, the inclusivity circle is leading on a listening project to build a picture of what inclusive looks like, in order to develop principles that will guide the project into the future. If you can help with policy proofing, desk research or community research, which could be related to making the park welcoming for the local community, we'd love to have a conversation.

Communications circle

The communications circle focuses on coordinating content for social media and the East London Waterworks Park website. It also responds to enquiries from members of the public and develops strategies to promote awareness of the project. If you have experience at any level in web development, communications strategy or social media, we should be especially interested in hearing from you.

Fundraising circle

The fundraising circle coordinates our fundraising efforts. It is thinking about future public-facing campaigns. It is also exploring grant opportunities and developing relationships with corporate bodies. If you can help with desk research into conservation and nature-based grant making and philanthropy, or if you have experience in corporate fundraising, crowdfunding, grant writing, philanthropy and PR, it would be great to hear from you.

Technology circle

The technology circle is responsible for providing technical support to the other circles. It is responsible for managing the website and the East London Waterworks Park's data more generally. If you are familiar with most of the following – HTML, PHP, CSS, Javascript, SQL – we should love to hear from you. We would also welcome people with knowledge and experience of the General Data Protection Regulations (GDPR).

How to get in touch

If you feel inspired and would like to join a circle, please contact us using the Contact us page. If you have an affinity with a particular circle, let us know. Alternatively, we can help you find your niche.
